    Contact Geometry

    1. The classification of Legendrian knots up to Legendrian isotopy is equivalent to the classification up to contact isotopy. This follows from isotropic isotopy extension lemma [Geiges, Theorem 2.6.2]. I'd like to understand the arguments as seen in [Legendrian and transversal knots - John B. Etnyre, Theorem 2.12]. See this question on MSE.

    2. Existence of contact structures on compact orientable 3-manifolds The current well known proof relies on Dehn surgery. I'd be happy to see a more algebraic proof of this fact. Another question of interest is, whether or not all contact structures on compact orientable 3-manifolds are coorientable.

    Topology

    1. Does there exist a topological manifold (with boundary), such that any injective continuous map into itself has at least TWO fixpoints?
    In the case of one fixpoint, take n-dimensional disk. If our manifold is smooth, has no boundary, is compact, nowhere vanishing vector field, then there are diffeos that have no FP. (think of flow)
